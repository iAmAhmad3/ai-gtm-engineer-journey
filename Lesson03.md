# Clay 101 - Lesson 03

**Topic:** Finding Companies with the Find Companies Dataset

---

## Objective

This lesson demonstrates how to build a targeted company list using Clay's **Find Companies** dataset.

Instead of importing random companies, Clay allows you to define your **Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)** and apply filters such as industry, company size, and location to generate highly relevant prospect lists.

---

## Why Use the Find Companies Dataset?

Successful outbound campaigns begin with the right audience.

The **Find Companies** dataset enables you to search millions of companies using structured filters, helping you build high-quality prospect lists that closely match your ICP.

---

## Step 1 — Create a Workbook

Before creating tables, organize your work by creating a **Workbook**.

A workbook acts as a container for related tables and workflows, making it easier to manage different campaigns or projects.

**Benefits**

- Better organization
- Easier collaboration
- Keeps related workflows together

---

## Step 2 — Use the Find Companies Dataset

Inside your workbook, create a new table and select the **Find Companies** source.

This dataset allows you to search for companies based on multiple attributes.

The goal is to build a list that closely matches your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP).

---

## Step 3 — Define Your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

An **Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)** defines the type of companies you want to target.

Rather than searching broadly, apply filters to identify companies that best fit your business goals.

Common ICP attributes include:

- Industry
- Company size
- Employee count
- Revenue
- Location
- Technologies used
- Funding stage

---

## Step 4 — Apply Filters

Clay allows you to narrow your search using multiple filters.

### Industry

Example:

- Software Development

This ensures only companies within the selected industry appear in your results.

---

### Employee Headcount

Example:

- Greater than 500 employees

This is useful when targeting mid-market or enterprise organizations.

---

### Location

Example:

- New York

Location filters help focus outreach on specific geographic markets.

---

## Step 5 — Review and Refine Results

After applying filters, preview the results before importing them.

If your list is:

**Too Small**

- Remove unnecessary filters
- Expand location
- Broaden company size

**Too Large**

- Add additional filters
- Narrow industries
- Specify locations
- Increase minimum headcount

Finding the right balance is an important part of building an effective prospect list.

---

## Step 6 — Import Companies

Once satisfied with your search results, import the companies into your Clay table.

**Important**

Avoid selecting **Default Enrichments** during the initial import.

Default enrichments consume credits, especially on trial accounts.

A better approach is to import the companies first and selectively enrich only the fields required for your workflow.

---

```text
        ↓

        ↓

        ↓

```

---

## Best Practices

- Always define your ICP before searching.
- Start with broad filters and refine gradually.
- Preview results before importing.
- Organize projects using Workbooks.
- Avoid unnecessary enrichments during import to conserve credits.
- Only enrich the data required for your campaign.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Workbooks help organize projects and workflows.
- The Find Companies dataset is the primary source for discovering companies in Clay.
- ICP-based filtering improves lead quality.
- Filters such as industry, employee count, and location help create targeted prospect lists.
- Preview results before importing.
- Skip default enrichments during import to avoid unnecessary credit usage.
